      Bradycardia can be caused by:
      • Heart tissue damage related to aging.
      • Damage to heart tissues from heart disease or heart attack.
      • Heart disorder present at birth (congenital heart defect)
      • Infection of heart tissue (myocarditis)
      • A complication of heart surgery.
      • Underactive thyroid gland (hypothyroidism)

      What causes low heart rate?
      Causes for bradycardia include: Problems with the sinoatrial (SA) node, sometimes called the heart's natural pacemaker. Problems in the conduction pathways of the heart that don't allow electrical impulses to pass properly from the atria to the ventricles. Metabolic problems such as hypothyroidism (low thyroid hormone)30-Sep-2016
